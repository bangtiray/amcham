AmCham Indonesia is a voluntary organization of professionals with commercials activities in Indonesia. Its central mission is to promote U.S.-Indonesia commercial relations and to serve its members as a key resource for information and business networking. First formed in 1971 as a committee, AmCham Indonesia has grown to hundreds of members representing more than 250 companies

By becoming a member of AmCham Indonesia, you benefit from business intelligence from leaders of the Indonesia business community, promotion of your firm, networking, access to business and political leaders, social activities and issue advocacy.

Membership Benefits

  • Direct notification of all AmCham meetings and events
  • Direct delivery of bi-weekly AmCham newsletter
  • Members-only access to the online directory and committee resources, such a presentations and minutes
  • Business and social networks in Jakarta, the Asia Pacific region, and the US.
  • Priority booking for the Annual Thanksgiving Day Golf Tournament
  • Opportunity to participate in AmCham Committees and working groups
  • Special member rate admission to AmCham events
  • Opportunities for engagement with the US Government representatives in Indonesia and visiting delegations
  • The annual directory, a critical resource for doing business in Indonesia
  • Special discounts at participating business in the AmCham Privileges Program

Membership Categories

General ( Company )

This category is open to companies and not-for-profit entities with US business interests; this includes:

  • Companies with international headquarters in the US or are traded in US stock markets and with business interests in Indonesia;
  • Companies in Indonesia that import American products as a significant part of their business;
  • Companies in Indonesia that export products to America as a significant part of their business;
  • Companies and organizations that provide goods and services to US companies and citizens in Indonesia
  • Overseas companies that have business interests in Indonesia

Each COR member would have a designated representative, who shall be the primary contact for the company and who receives the COR ballot.
